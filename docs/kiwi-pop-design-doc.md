# KIWI POP
## Brand Design Document v1.0

> *A functional, sugar-free, vegan lollipop brand for the cross-scene underground.*
> Not candy. A vibe-accessory that does something.

---

## 01. THE THESIS

Kiwi Pop sits at the intersection of five aesthetic universes the brief calls out:

| Reference | What we steal from it |
|---|---|
| **Gorillaz** | Cartoon-as-band, fictional persona-driven world, mixed-media collage, the idea that the brand IS a crew of characters |
| **Cyberpunk Pop** | Glitch type, scan lines, Japanese kanji as ornament, neon on black, chromatic aberration, "future-decay" texture |
| **Asian Street** | Density, layered signage, vending-machine reds and signage yellows, kawaii contrasted with grit |
| **Rave Pop** | Saturated UV color, gel/liquid renders, sweat-shine, after-dark energy, after-3am-hour color logic |
| **Flirty Playful Party Underground** | Lips, tongues, candy-as-sex, wink-wink coding, bratty confidence, lowercase-everything voice |

These don't blend into a smoothie. They are kept as **distinct layers stacked on top of each other** - the way the Pinterest moodboard you uploaded is composed. The brand looks like a board, not a single image.

The product question Kiwi Pop answers:
> *"What do I put in my mouth at 1am at the warehouse, in line at the festival, on the dance floor, in the Uber home - that gives me energy, doesn't wreck my body, and signals my taste before I open my mouth?"*

## 02. POSITIONING

**Category:** Functional confection / mood enhancer
**Product reality:** Sugar-free, vegan lollipop with a functional ingredient (caffeine, adaptogen, nootropic - flavor-line dependent)
**Cultural position:** The first lollipop that earned its place in the going-out fit

**One-liner:** *Kiwi Pop. Suck on the future.*

**Three-line elevator:**
> Kiwi Pop is a sugar-free, vegan, functional lollipop made for the dance floor. Each flavor is a feeling, each flavor does a thing. It is candy for people who don't eat candy.

**Why "Kiwi":**
- The fruit reads green/sour/playful - a built-in flavor and color anchor
- Phonetically soft and feminine, balances the aggression of the visual world
- Suggestive without being crude (kiwi = flirt-coded fruit in pop culture)
- Single-syllable, ownable, internationally pronounceable

## 03. THE WORLD (Brand Universe)

Kiwi Pop is not a logo on a wrapper. It's a **world** with characters, locations, and lore. Borrowing from the Gorillaz playbook:

### The Crew (illustrated mascots, not real people)

These appear across packaging, IG, the site, merch:

1. **KIWI** - the green-haired front character. Eternal 22. Looks bored at her own party. Always has a pop in her mouth.
2. **NEKO** - cyberpunk DJ. Visor over eyes. Plays the after-after.
3. **LIP** - the messenger. All mouth, all gloss, never seen above the chin. Speaks in lipstick smudges.
4. **GLITCH** - the chaotic one. Pixelates when emotional. Currently dating LIP.

These are sketched in the Asian-street/anime-illustration style of your moodboard (image 3 and image 4 references). They appear:
- On every flavor's wrapper as a "starring" credit
- As stickers in every order
- As IG story characters reacting to product drops
- On the landing page as an animated cast lineup

### The Setting

**"NEW SHIBUYA, 4:17 AM"** - a fictional district where Kiwi Pop is from. Think Akihabara meets Berghain meets a bodega. This setting recurs in:
- Photography backdrops (vending machines, neon, wet asphalt)
- Drop announcements ("delivered to NEW SHIBUYA tonight only")
- Map graphics on the site

## 04. VOICE & WRITING

### Voice principles

- **Lowercase by default.** Caps are a weapon, used sparingly for emphasis or shouting.
- **Short sentences. Then longer ones that loop back.**
- **Bratty confidence, not aggression.** Kiwi Pop never tries hard. It already won.
- **Wink without explaining the wink.** Innuendo is left for the reader to catch.
- **Functional claims are deadpan.** "200mg caffeine. zero sugar. no notes." Don't oversell the science.
- **Japanese phrases as ornament**, not as content. (Never use language Claude can't verify is correct - keep to common, recognizable phrases.)

### Voice samples

❌ "Energize your night with our delicious sugar-free lollipops!"
✅ "sugar's dead. long live the lick."

❌ "Kiwi Pop is the perfect treat for parties."
✅ "we made a lollipop for the people who never sit down."

❌ "Try our exciting new flavor!"
✅ "new drop. tastes like trouble. 800 made. don't sleep."

### Forbidden words
*delicious, treat, sweet treat, yummy, indulgent, guilt-free, cheat day, naughty (too cringe), wellness, self-care, journey*

### Encouraged words
*lick, suck, drop, after, nasty (sparingly), filthy (sparingly), open, late, nocturnal, the floor, the line, the after, sticky, glossy, wet*

## 05. VISUAL SYSTEM

### Color

A two-system palette: **Base** is the room. **Drops** are the spotlight.

**BASE (always present, structural)**
- `--ink: #050505` (near-black, the void)
- `--paper: #f4f0e8` (warm off-white, the only soft thing)
- `--bone: #d8d2c4` (mid-neutral for layering)

**DROPS (one dominates per flavor / per drop / per page)**
- `--kiwi: #a8ff3c` (signature green - the brand's anchor)
- `--gloss: #ff2d6f` (lip pink - flirt mode)
- `--cherry: #ff1f3d` (deep red - signage red, danger)
- `--electric: #00f0ff` (cyber cyan - rave mode)
- `--bruise: #6a00ff` (UV purple - late night)
- `--sodium: #ffce1f` (signage yellow - daylight, contrast)

**Rule:** Every layout uses `--ink` + `--paper` + ONE drop color dominant + ONE drop color accent. Never more than two drops per surface. Two drops + black + cream = always works. Five colors = chaos and we lose.

### Typography

Three faces, used with discipline:

1. **Display - chunky distorted sans** (e.g., *Migra*, *Editorial New Italic*, *Druk Wide*, or a free alternative like *Bricolage Grotesque*). Used for the brand wordmark and oversized hero type. Should feel like it's been pressed into vinyl.

2. **UI / Body - clean technical mono or grotesk** (e.g., *JetBrains Mono*, *IBM Plex Mono*, or *Space Mono*). Used for everything operational: prices, ingredients, drop dates, button text. Brings the "spec sheet / vending machine display" feeling.

3. **Accent - cyber-display kanji-styled font OR hand-scrawled marker.** Used 5% of the time for stickers, callouts, "limited" badges. Provides chaos.

**No:** Inter, Helvetica, Arial, Roboto, Poppins. These are the death of personality.

### Layout principles

The site and packaging use these recurring moves, pulled from your reference images:

- **Z-axis stacking.** Things overlap. Stickers on top of photos. Type on top of type. Nothing sits politely in its lane. (See moodboard image 1 - the techno geisha layout)
- **Hard frames + ragged edges.** Corner brackets, scan-line dividers, but with torn-paper or glitch breaks.
- **The vertical kanji column.** A column of Japanese characters running down the side, used as decoration, not content.
- **Sticker chaos.** Floating ornamental elements that look stuck on after the fact - barcodes, "limited", "21+", star bursts, tiny mascot heads.
- **Asymmetric grids.** No 3-column hero. Use 12-column with intentional emptiness on one side.
- **Photo treatments:** chromatic aberration on faces, wet/gloss highlights, blue-rim-light on warm skin, slight VHS scanline overlay.

### Motion principles

- **Loading states are theatre.** A pop dissolving, a tongue extending, a "buffering" glitch.
- **Hover = flirt.** Buttons lick, wink, blink. Subtle, not silly.
- **The cursor reacts.** Cursor leaves a glossy wet trail or pulls a small kiwi behind it on key pages.
- **Page transitions: shutter.** Like a vending-machine slot opening.

### Texture & detail

Every surface has at least one of:
- Fine grain noise (5-8% opacity)
- Subtle scan lines on dark surfaces
- Holographic gradient on accents (only on premium/limited drops)
- Slight chromatic split on hero imagery (red/cyan offset, 1-2px)

## 06. PRODUCT LINE (Initial Drop)

Each flavor = a feeling = a function = a drop color.

| Flavor | Feeling | Function | Color |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GREEN HOUR** | "the night is starting" | 80mg caffeine, L-theanine | `--kiwi` |
| **GLOSS** | "the night is on" | 150mg caffeine, ginseng | `--gloss` (pink) |
| **AFTER** | "after the after" | ashwagandha, magnesium, no caffeine | `--bruise` (UV purple) |
| **STATIC** | "stay awake or die trying" | 200mg caffeine, taurine | `--electric` (cyan) |

Each flavor wrapper features a different mascot from the Crew. Each has its own micro-site / drop page. Each is released as a "drop" with quantity caps and dates - building scarcity and culture.

## 07. PACKAGING DIRECTION (Concepts)

**Single pop:** Foil wrapper, full-bleed flavor color, printed mascot illustration, technical-mono ingredient panel on the back. Wrapper reads like a poster - people will save them.

**4-pack:** Black matte box, slide-out vending-machine-style tray. One of each flavor. Box doubles as a stash-able object on a vanity.

**Limited/collab drops:** Holographic foil, numbered (e.g., 247/800), serialized, sold only via the site for one night.

## 08. THE LANDING PAGE BRIEF

The landing page must answer in under 8 seconds:
1. **What is this?** (a lollipop brand)
2. **What does it feel like?** (the answer is the page itself)
3. **Why is it different?** (functional, sugar-free, vegan, cross-scene)
4. **What do I do next?** (sign up for the drop / shop)

Page sections:
1. **Hero** - oversized brand name, glitch treatment, mascot peeking, drop counter
2. **The Manifesto** - 3-4 lines of voice. Black background, big type.
3. **The Drop** - the 4 flavors as cards, each its own color world
4. **The Crew** - mascot lineup (the Gorillaz move)
5. **The Spec** - functional ingredient breakdown in technical-mono, deadpan
6. **The List** - email signup framed as "get on the list" (club language)

Tone of motion: **arrives loud, settles into rhythm, never apologizes.**

## 09. WHAT TO DO NEXT (Shipping Notes)

This is the design/brand foundation. To get to actually selling lollipops, the immediate next moves are:

1. **Lock the wordmark.** Get one custom-drawn KIWI POP wordmark (~$300-800 from a freelance type designer on Working Not Working or even Fiverr's top tier). Everything else is built on this.
2. **Commission the mascot illustrations.** One illustrator, four characters, character-sheet style. Find someone in the anime/streetwear illustration scene on Cara or Behance. Budget $1,500-3,500 for the set.
3. **Source the actual lollipop manufacturer.** Sugar-free + vegan + functional ingredients = small list. Look at private-label confectioners who already work with functional brands. This is the long-pole item - start now.
4. **Build a Shopify store using this design language**, not a generic Shopify theme. The landing page demo is the proof of concept.
5. **Soft-launch to a 50-person list** before going big. Drop a test flavor in one city.

**What NOT to do next:** spend 6 more months refining the design system without a manufactured product to sell. The design is a vehicle for the product. Get the product real.
