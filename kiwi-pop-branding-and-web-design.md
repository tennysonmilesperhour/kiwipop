# PART ONE — THE NEW DIRECTION (CENTER OF GRAVITY)

This mood board is an absolute electric dream. It's **"Tokyo at 3:00 AM in the year 2099"** — heavy on the Cyber-Pop, Vaporwave, and Glitch-core vibes.

Everything below this section flows from this direction. Older brand notes have been kept where they still fit and adjusted where they don't.

---

## 01. THE PALETTE: "ATOMIC NEON"

We aren't just doing "bright" — we're doing **bioluminescent**.

### Primary
- **Electric Lime** — the "Kiwi" core
- **Neon Cyan** — the "Pop" core

### Accents
- Magenta-pink
- Ultraviolet
- "Oil Slick" iridescence

### Foundation
- **Deep Midnight Black**
- **"Terminal" Grey** — to make the neons vibrate

### Working hex values to start from
*(These will get tuned once we test on packaging substrate and screen, but they're the starting palette in code.)*

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `--midnight` | `#050510` | Deep black foundation, the void |
| `--terminal` | `#1a1a22` | Mid-grey, layering, UI panels |
| `--lime` | `#a8ff3c` | Electric Lime, signature green |
| `--cyan` | `#00f0ff` | Neon Cyan, the "Pop" |
| `--magenta` | `#ff2d8a` | Magenta-pink accent |
| `--ultraviolet` | `#7b2dff` | UV purple accent |
| `--oilslick` | gradient | Iridescent treatment for premium drops |

**The rule:** Every layout uses `--midnight` + `--terminal` + ONE neon dominant + ONE neon accent. Two neons + black + grey = always vibrates. Five neons = noise.

The cream/paper "warm off-white" used in earlier drafts is **out**. Kiwi Pop lives at night.

## 02. THE CORE MOTIFS: "THE ALL-SEEING EYE"

There is a massive recurring theme on the board of **eyes** — macro shots of irises, eyes with galaxy patterns, digital visors.

**Concept:** kiwi pop isn't just a snack — it's a **vision**.

### Application
- A hyper-detailed, iridescent **"kiwi-seed eye"** as a secondary logo or limited-edition packaging graphic. The kiwi seed cluster *is* the iris.
- Plays beautifully off the gecko-iris-slit anatomical interest already in your world — same fascination with strange, perfect biological geometry.
- Mascot variants where eyes are replaced by visors, scan lines, or full galaxy renders.
- Site-level: cursor leaves a glossy iris trail. Scroll-triggered "eye opens" transitions on key sections.

---

## 03. DESIGN STYLE: "LIQUID CYBERPUNK"

The board balances hard-edged technology with fluid, organic shapes — melting faces, dripping chrome, glowing jellyfish.

### Typography
- **Display:** High-contrast sans-serif (Helvetica or Inter weight extremes — ultralight next to ultrablack) for clean structural type
- **Pair with:** Aggressive, glitched-out Japanese katakana
- **Mono:** "Hacker" monospace (JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex Mono, Space Mono) for spec sheets, ingredient panels, drop dates, prices
- **Accent:** Distorted display face for the wordmark itself — should feel pressed into vinyl or printed on a vending machine

*Note: the older brand doc said "no Helvetica/Inter — death of personality." That rule was for a softer, paper-and-ink direction. In the Atomic Neon world, Helvetica Neue Black against neon-on-black actually rips — it becomes the clean control surface that lets the chaos around it read.*

### Imagery
- 90s-style anime cel-shading (think **Gorillaz** or **Akira**)
- Mixed with hyper-realistic 3D renders
- Chromatic aberration, scan lines, VHS bleed
- Wet/gloss highlights, blue-rim-light on warm skin
- Macro biology shots (irises, kiwi seeds, gecko eyes) treated as cyber-objects

## 04. TACTICAL NEXT STEPS

Based on this energy, here is how we apply the direction to actual assets:

### 1. Packaging
- **Matte black canisters or wrappers** with **UV-reactive spot gloss**
- When the lights go down at the rave / party, the logo should **activate**
- This is a real, sourceable print finish — UV-reactive inks under blacklight are standard at specialty printers
- Each flavor gets one neon as the dominant under-color, with the matte black on top breaking only where the logo and graphics live

### 2. The "Crew" Mascots
- Lean into **Gorillaz-style character design** for **Kiwi**, **Neko**, **Lip**, and **Glitch**
- Tattoos, tech-wear, visors — exactly the vocabulary on the mood board
- One illustrator for the full set, character-sheet style
- They appear on packaging, in stickers shipped with every order, as IG story characters, and as the animated cast lineup on the site

### 3. The App / Shopify Storefront UI
- **Dark mode is a must** — there is no light mode for Kiwi Pop
- "Neon-on-black" logic throughout
- **Glowing borders** on interactive elements (1-2px outer glow on hover, sharper on focus)
- **Glitch transitions** between pages and on add-to-cart — short, intentional, never longer than ~400ms or it gets annoying
- Shopify storefront should not look like a Shopify storefront. Custom theme, custom liquid templates, custom checkout where allowed

## 05. THE THESIS

Kiwi Pop sits at the intersection of five aesthetic universes — now all routed through the Tokyo-2099 lens:

| Reference | What we steal from it |
|---|---|
| **Gorillaz** | Cartoon-as-band, fictional persona-driven world, mixed-media collage, the brand IS a crew of characters |
| **Cyber-Pop / Vaporwave / Glitch-core** | Glitch type, scan lines, Japanese katakana as ornament, neon on black, chromatic aberration, "future-decay" texture |
| **Asian Street** | Density, layered signage, vending-machine reds and signage yellows, kawaii contrasted with grit |
| **Rave Pop** | Saturated UV color, gel/liquid renders, sweat-shine, after-dark energy |
| **Flirty Playful Party Underground** | Lips, tongues, candy-as-sex, wink-wink coding, bratty confidence, lowercase voice |

These don't blend into a smoothie. They are kept as **distinct layers stacked on top of each other** — the brand looks like a board, not a single image.

The product question Kiwi Pop answers:
> *"What do I put in my mouth at 1am at the warehouse, in line at the festival, on the dance floor, in the Uber home — that gives me energy, doesn't wreck my body, and signals my taste before I open my mouth?"*

## 06. POSITIONING

- **Category:** Functional confection / mood enhancer
- **Product reality:** Sugar-free, vegan lollipop with a functional ingredient (caffeine, adaptogen, nootropic — flavor-line dependent)
- **Cultural position:** The first lollipop that earned its place in the going-out fit

**One-liner:** *Kiwi Pop. Suck on the future.*

**Three-line elevator:**
> Kiwi Pop is a sugar-free, vegan, functional lollipop made for the dance floor. Each flavor is a feeling, each flavor does a thing. It is candy for people who don't eat candy.

**Why "Kiwi":**
- The fruit reads green/sour/playful — built-in flavor and color anchor
- Phonetically soft and feminine, balances the aggression of the visual world
- Suggestive without being crude
- Single-syllable, ownable, internationally pronounceable

## 07. THE WORLD (Brand Universe)

### The Crew (illustrated mascots, not real people)

These appear across packaging, IG, the site, merch:

1. **KIWI** — the green-haired front character. Eternal 22. Looks bored at her own party. Always has a pop in her mouth.
2. **NEKO** — cyberpunk DJ. Visor over eyes. Plays the after-after.
3. **LIP** — the messenger. All mouth, all gloss, never seen above the chin. Speaks in lipstick smudges.
4. **GLITCH** — the chaotic one. Pixelates when emotional. Currently dating LIP.

Drawn in the Asian-street / anime-illustration style of the mood board — Gorillaz lineage, tech-wear, tattoos, visors. They appear:
- On every flavor's wrapper as a "starring" credit
- As stickers in every order
- As IG story characters reacting to product drops
- On the landing page as an animated cast lineup

### The Setting

**"NEW SHIBUYA, 4:17 AM"** — a fictional district where Kiwi Pop is from. Akihabara meets Berghain meets a bodega. This setting recurs in:
- Photography backdrops (vending machines, neon, wet asphalt)
- Drop announcements ("delivered to NEW SHIBUYA tonight only")
- Map graphics on the site

## 08. VOICE & WRITING

### Voice principles
- **Lowercase by default.** Caps are a weapon, used sparingly.
- **Short sentences. Then longer ones that loop back.**
- **Bratty confidence, not aggression.** Kiwi Pop never tries hard. It already won.
- **Wink without explaining the wink.** Innuendo is left for the reader to catch.
- **Functional claims are deadpan.** "200mg caffeine. zero sugar. no notes."
- **Japanese phrases as ornament,** not as content.

### Voice samples
❌ "Energize your night with our delicious sugar-free lollipops!"
✅ "sugar's dead. long live the lick."

❌ "Kiwi Pop is the perfect treat for parties."
✅ "we made a lollipop for the people who never sit down."

❌ "Try our exciting new flavor!"
✅ "new drop. tastes like trouble. 800 made. don't sleep."

### Forbidden words
*delicious, treat, sweet treat, yummy, indulgent, guilt-free, cheat day, naughty, wellness, self-care, journey*

### Encouraged words
*lick, suck, drop, after, nasty (sparingly), filthy (sparingly), open, late, nocturnal, the floor, the line, the after, sticky, glossy, wet*

## 09. LAYOUT PRINCIPLES (carry forward, refit for neon)

The site and packaging use these recurring moves:

- **Z-axis stacking.** Things overlap. Stickers on top of photos. Type on top of type. Nothing sits politely in its lane.
- **Hard frames + ragged edges.** Corner brackets, scan-line dividers, glitch breaks.
- **The vertical katakana column.** A column of Japanese characters running down the side, used as decoration, not content.
- **Sticker chaos.** Floating ornamental elements that look stuck on after the fact — barcodes, "limited", "21+", star bursts, tiny mascot heads.
- **Asymmetric grids.** No 3-column hero. 12-column with intentional emptiness on one side.
- **Photo treatments:** chromatic aberration on faces, wet/gloss highlights, blue-rim-light on warm skin, slight VHS scanline overlay.

### Motion principles
- **Loading states are theatre.** A pop dissolving, a tongue extending, a buffering glitch.
- **Hover = flirt.** Buttons lick, wink, blink. Subtle, not silly.
- **The cursor reacts.** Cursor leaves a glossy wet trail or pulls a small kiwi-seed eye behind it on key pages.
- **Page transitions: shutter.** Like a vending-machine slot opening.

### Texture & detail
Every surface has at least one of:
- Fine grain noise (5-8% opacity)
- Subtle scan lines on dark surfaces
- Holographic / oil-slick gradient on accents (only on premium / limited drops)
- Slight chromatic split on hero imagery (red/cyan offset, 1-2px)

Each flavor = a feeling = a function = a neon.

| Flavor | Feeling | Function | Neon |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GREEN HOUR** | "the night is starting" | 80mg caffeine, L-theanine | `--lime` |

| **AFTER** | "after the after" | ashwagandha, magnesium, no caffeine | `--ultraviolet` |

## 11. PACKAGING DIRECTION

- **Single pop:** Matte black foil wrapper, full-bleed flavor neon underneath, UV-reactive spot gloss on the logo, printed mascot, technical-mono ingredient panel on the back.
- **4-pack:** Black matte box, slide-out vending-machine-style tray. One of each flavor. Box doubles as a stash-able object on a vanity.
- **Limited / collab drops:** Oil-slick holographic foil, numbered (e.g., 247/800), serialized, sold only via the site for one night.

---

## 12. THE LANDING PAGE BRIEF

The landing page must answer in under 8 seconds:
1. **What is this?** (a lollipop brand)
2. **What does it feel like?** (the answer is the page itself)
3. **Why is it different?** (functional, sugar-free, vegan, cross-scene)
4. **What do I do next?** (sign up for the drop / shop)

### Page sections
1. **Hero** — oversized brand name, glitch treatment, mascot peeking, drop counter
2. **The Manifesto** — 3-4 lines of voice, midnight background, big type
3. **The Drop** — the 4 flavors as cards, each its own neon world
4. **The Crew** — mascot lineup (the Gorillaz move)
5. **The Spec** — functional ingredient breakdown in mono, deadpan
6. **The List** — email signup framed as "get on the list" (club language)

**Tone of motion:** *arrives loud, settles into rhythm, never apologizes.*

# PART THREE — SHIPPING NOTES

This is the design / brand foundation. To get to actually selling lollipops, the immediate next moves stay the same as before — and stay in this order.

1. **Lock the wordmark.** One custom-drawn KIWI POP wordmark. Everything else is built on this. Budget: ~$300-800 from a freelance type designer.
2. **Commission the mascot illustrations.** One illustrator, four characters, character-sheet style. Budget: $1,500-3,500.
3. **Source the actual lollipop manufacturer.** Sugar-free + vegan + functional ingredients = small list of co-packers. **This is the long-pole item — start now, in parallel with everything else.**
4. **Build the Shopify store using this design language**, not a generic Shopify theme. The landing page demo is the proof of concept.
5. **Soft-launch to a 50-person list** before going big. Drop a test flavor in one city.
